## Outcomes per branch

### B1 — `final.pt` is a partial winner, ONLY at high per-step budget

**v17-E.50 + final.pt** is the new project SOTA on the alignment metrics:

- correct-part recall **0.292** vs prior best_contact-E.50 0.275 (**+1.7 pp**) — highest ever recorded on a single-sample raw metric (no oracle).
- same-part local error **36.11 cm** vs prior 39.02 cm (**−2.91 cm**) — also project-best.
- IoU 0.507 vs 0.504 — wash.
- contact 16.86 cm carries the same metric-gaming caveat as prior E.50 (< GT VQ roundtrip 18.47).

**v17-E.20 + final.pt** is a regression:

- correct-part 0.241 vs prior best_contact-E.20 0.264 (**−2.3 pp**).
- contact 19.69 vs 18.62 (+1.07 cm).
- local 43.35 vs 42.09 (+1.26 cm).

Mechanism (interpretation): `final.pt` has a slightly broader raw
distribution than `best_contact.pt` (raw contact 28.05 vs 26.79 cm;
PROGRESS already noted final.pt has 0.199 correct-part recall on raw
generation vs best_contact's 0.176). The wider raw distribution requires
more per-step budget to pull onto the contact target. At per_step=20 the
budget is insufficient; at per_step=50 the budget catches up and the raw
correct-part advantage of final.pt translates into a guided correct-part
win.

**Decision**: ship `v17-E.50 + final.pt` as the new default (subject to
visual review, see §Next steps).

So `part_margin` and `segment_consistency` are NOT the lever. The B2 hypothesis (re-diagnosis F-5: "the visual right-area-wrong-patch failure is directly explained by the missing wrong-part penalty") is **REFUTED at inference time**, even though the same terms work at training time.

### B3 — residual drift is the actual ceiling (and explains B2's failure)

Mean |residual_drift| per condition:

| variant | drift mean (cm) | drift max (cm) |
|---|---:|---:|
| B2 sanity (pm=0, sc=0) | 5.93 | 49.86 |
| B1 E.20 final.pt | 5.88 | 53.67 |
| B2 pm=0.5 | 7.59 | 52.00 |
| B2 pm=1.0 (alone) | 8.57 | 71.46 |
| B1 E.50 final.pt | 8.43 | 104.12 |
| B2 pm=2.0 | **11.86** | 73.84 |

Two key observations:

1. **Baseline drift is ~6 cm**. Even without any aux loss, the per-step
   optimiser's converged inner-loop loss differs from the final
   post-residual-rerun loss by ~6 cm on average. The `/restart §B3`
   threshold for "approximation acceptable" was < 1 cm; we are firmly
   in the > 5 cm regime where mid-loop residual refresh is justified.

2. **Drift scales with part_margin weight**. pm=2.0 doubles drift (11.86
   vs 5.93 cm). The mechanism: `part_margin` makes the inner-loop
   optimiser more aggressive (forces additional base-token flips to
   satisfy the wrong-part penalty), which makes the post-guidance base
   distribution diverge further from the naive baseline base
   distribution that `baseline_residual_emb_sum` was computed for.
   Residual rerun then produces residuals that are even further from
   what the optimiser saw → more drift → finer optimiser tuning gets
   washed out.

This is the unifying explanation: **B2 fails because the aux terms
amplify the same drift that already caps per-step's ceiling**. The
training-time terms work because training has end-to-end gradient
through the ACTUAL residual transformer; inference per-step optimises
against a frozen residual approximation.

### N2 — Implement B3' = mid-loop residual refresh (~1 day code + 4 h server)

Hypothesis: drift mean ≥ 5 cm caps per-step's ceiling. Refresh
`baseline_residual_emb_sum` every K MaskGIT steps by re-running the
residual transformer on the in-progress base tokens. Cost: K extra
residual-transformer forwards per clip (negligible — residual generation
is ~1 s/clip).

API: `--per-step-residual-refresh-every N` (default 0 = disabled =
back-compat). `N=1` refreshes every MaskGIT step (most aggressive).

After B3' lands, **re-run B2 sweep on top of B3'-active**. Hypothesis:
with drift < 2 cm, part_margin / segment_consistency may flip from
negative to positive. If yes: B3' + B2 stacks to ship the part-aware
recipe. If still negative: lift the inference saturation claim with
confidence and move to B4.
